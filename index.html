US Air passenger demand

Predicting the passenger volumn of US core air travel markets




Let's start!

Passengers and destinations

Based on the report of the 1000 largest domestic city-pair markets in US, the information regards every origin city, including the number of passengers per day and the destination cities is presented in the map.

Process

Data

Sources: U.S. Department of Transportation
U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis

Prediction

A random forest regressor

Cluster analysis

K-Means clustering


Features

Origin & Destination

GDP, income, population, employment

Flight

Fare, distance

Prediction

The number of each city-pair market has a different degree of correlation with the features mentioned before. A random forest regressor is used to predict the number of passengers, with R2 = 0.85.

Clustering

After performing an analysis of feature importance, the product of the Real GPD of origin and destination cities and the ticket fare are the two most informative features. A k-means clustering with on the these variables with k = 3, returns three categories of city-pair markets.
1. Large market: high GDP
2. Small market: low GDP (constrain), low fare
3. Small market: high GDP, high fare (constrain)
